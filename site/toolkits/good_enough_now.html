Phase 1 of 4
Anchor the session
Three questions. Don't skip — they're the fence that stops your brain from solving everything at once.
What ONE decision must this session produce?
Not "design the system" — too big. One specific call.
What zoom level are you working at today?
Pick one. "All of them" means scope is already too large.
Who needs to agree with the output?
Too many people = scope is too large.
Phase 2 of 4
Draw the fence
Explicitly name what you are NOT solving today. Every item here is a rabbit hole you're allowed to skip.
What constraints are FIXED? (don't question these today)
Existing stack, team size, latency SLA, cost ceiling — your walls.
What are you explicitly NOT deciding today?
Write as many as you want. This is your TBD licence — drop these without guilt.
One sentence: what does "done" look like?
If you had this at session end, you'd feel finished.
Phase 3 of 4 · You're in the session now
Capture & keep moving
Brain spots a rabbit hole? Dump it below and move on. Don't solve it now.

Phase 4 of 4
What did you decide?
Close the loop. One decision, one next step. Everything else is filed as TBD.
State the ONE decision in one sentence.
Can't write it in one sentence? You haven't decided yet — go back and narrow.
ONE next action. Who owns it?
A name, not a team. A day, not a sprint.
